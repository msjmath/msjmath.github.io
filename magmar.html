About
 

The Mission San Jose High School Math Club is proud to run MagMaR, a middle school math competition for motivated math competitors in the Bay Area from 6th to 8th grade. The contest introduces a unique contest format and an appealing theme to traditional math competitions.

What: A math tournament with a unique flavor
Who: Students in 6th to 8th grade, in teams of up to 5
Where: Magnolia Science Academy in Santa Clara. Address is: 2720 Sonoma Place, Santa Clara, CA 95051
When: On Sunday March 29, 2015, from 9:00 to 3:30 (NOTE: We have moved the date due to time conflicts with MathCounts)

Email us at magmarmath@gmail.com!

To the "educated" middle-schooler, the abbreviation for the Mission Math Regional (MagMaR) seems to share its name with a certain Pokemon. We hope to make this contest as exciting for its participants as possible, while providing an instructive opportunity for everyone. While the details of the involvement of Pokemon with the contest theme will not be revealed until the contest itself, we assure that no math problem will require any knowledge of Pokemon.

We hope this contest will be a unique experience for you!

Rules

Events

  • Individual Round: This round consists of 20 short-answer questions to be answered in 60 minutes. Each student must work individually, and each problem is worth 3 points.
  • Wild Round: This round consists of 40 short-answer problems with a time limit of 20 minutes. Each student must work individually, and each problem is worth 1 point.
  • Team Round: The team round is a 40 minute team test with 15 questions. Students on a team may work together. Each problem is worth 10 points.
  • Blitz Round: This round consists of 30 problems with a time limit of 60 minutes. Students on a team may work together. At the start of the round, the team is given Problems 1-3. There are three ways to get another problem: you can get a question right, choose to skip a question, or submit a question unsuccessfully three times. A team may only have three problems at any given time and may make a maximum of 3 submissions on any given problem.

    A team may submit a problem at any time during the round by handing it in to the collection proctor. Grading is immediate, and scores will be posted live on a public scoreboard. A problem is worth 5 points on first submission, 4 points on second submission, and 3 points on third submission for a maximum of 150 points.

Food

Food options are limited near Magnolia Science Academy, and placing a group pizza order with us will be the most convenient option. Please select your pizza order on the registration form, indicating the number of slices of each type of pizza. Pizza is $1.50 per slice, and the options are cheese, pepperoni, meat, hawaiian, combination, and veggie.

During registration, tickets will be given to teams who have purchased lunch, and must be presented in order to receive the lunch. Tickets cannot be replaced if misplaced.

Materials

Pencils, pens, erasers, and straightedges are allowed for each round. In addition, scratch paper will be provided by the MagMaR Coordinators. However, the following items are explicitly not allowed: calculators, slide rules, books, graph paper, computers, electronic devices, graph paper, protractors, and compasses. Please ask your proctor if you would like to use any other materials.

Answer Format

Unless otherwise specified, all answers should be expressed exactly.
  • Fractional answers should be written in simplest form. Fractions greater than 1 may be expressed as either improper fractions or mixed numbers.
  • Numbers with radicals should be expressed in simplest radical form.
  • Denominators with radicals do not need to be rationalized, but the radicals must be simplified.
  • Answers involving pi or other irrational constants should not be approximated. Instead, answers should be submitted in terms of the constant.
  • Answers that should be expressed as ordered pairs should be written in the form (a, b).
  • Both decimal and fractional answers are acceptable as long as they are exact.
  • Diagrams, if provided, are not necessarily drawn to scale.
Any simplification instructions specifically mentioned in any test problem overrides any of these aforementioned rules for that problem only.

Scoring/Awards

Each student's score will be computed by adding their Individual Round score (maximum 60 points) to their Wild Round score (maximum 40 points), for a total of 100 possible points.

Each team's score will be calculated by taking three-fifths the sum of the individual scores of the team members (300 possible points) and adding the Team round score (150 possible points) and Blitz round score (150 possible points) for a total of 600 possible points.

Prizes will be given to the teams with the highest team scores and the students in each grade with the highest individual scores. In addition, recognition will be given to the highest scoring teams in each round.
